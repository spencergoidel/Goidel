The Trump Realignment

Spencer Goidel and Kirby Goidel

Forthcoming: Palgrave Macmillan

The Trump Realignment argues that focusing only on Trump as a democratic threat misses a second, politically consequential story: Trump as a transformative party leader who identified gaps between elite consensus and mass sentiment, and used them to reshape Republican electoral strategy.

Core Themes

  • How Trump captured and remade the GOP coalition, organizationally and electorally.
  • How the diploma divide and class-based cleavages reorganized partisan competition.
  • How race, business politics, immigration, and trade became central axes of the new alignment.
  • Why these shifts likely outlast Trump and define American party conflict for years ahead.

For Readers

This book is written for scholars, students, journalists, and practitioners seeking a theory-informed and evidence-based account of contemporary party-system change in the United States.
